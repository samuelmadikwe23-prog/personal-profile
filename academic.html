MY ACADEMIC JOURNEY

I began my academic journey at SSKB Kindergarten, where the foundations of learning were first laid. From there, I moved to Newton International, a private school where I did fairly well academically and discovered swimming, a sport I pursued for three years. Later, I transitioned to Ben Thema Primary School, a public school that was full of fun and new experiences. While I didn’t dive deeply into sports here, I joined the table tennis club in my later years, which gave me a taste of discipline and competition.

In 2018, I entered Bokamoso Junior Secondary School. To be honest, nothing significant happened for me there — it was a place where many tried to figure out who they were, but I already knew myself; I just hadn’t yet developed the confidence to show it. After a year and a half, I moved to Embakwe High School in Zimbabwe, and this was a turning point. My joy for school skyrocketed, my grades improved, and I finally felt genuine. I met people who resonated with me, joined the rugby team, and played for a year and a half, creating unforgettable memories. It was also here that I got my first taste of computer science, and it stuck with me deeply. Unfortunately, the experience was cut short when COVID-19 struck, and I had to return home.

Back in Botswana, I enrolled at Oriel High School. While it wasn’t the best environment academically, I made good friends and finished with 38 points. Still hungry for more, I pleaded to rewrite, but 2023 didn’t turn out as I had hoped — it felt like a wasted year . Determined not to give up, I tried again in 2024, balancing studies with work. It was tough, but I managed to finish with 43 points, proving my persistence and resilience.

Now, as I pursue my BSc in Computer Systems Engineering at Botswana Accountancy College, I look back and realize that the hunger I had for a perfect high school certificate wasn’t truly worth it. What mattered most were the lessons I learned outside the classroom — the resilience, the teamwork, the discipline, and the vision I developed along the way. Those experiences shaped me far more than any grade, and they continue to fuel my ambition for the future.
